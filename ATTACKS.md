# Failed and constrained approaches

Nothing has been attempted in this repo yet. The entries below are *seed knowledge* — constraints inherited from the literature and back-of-envelope estimates, recorded so no session wastes effort rediscovering them. Recompute estimates before trusting them.

## Structural constraints on any proof (from the literature)

- **The +1 is essential.** ErGy99 showed balanced r-colourings of $K_{r^2}$ exist for infinitely many $r$. Any proof of the conjecture must exploit the difference between $r^2$ and $r^2+1$ crucially; an argument that never distinguishes them cannot succeed.
- **Intuition from r=2 misleads.** $N(2)=5=2^2+1$: the conjecture's statement is genuinely false for $r=2$, and only starts at $r=3$. Arguments that would apply equally at $r=2$ prove too much.

## Encoding-size sanity estimates (r=5, not yet attempted)

- One-hot SAT encoding: $\binom{26}{2}=325$ edges × 5 colours = 1625 boolean variables (+ exactly-one constraints per edge). Balancedness: for each of the $\binom{26}{6}=230{,}230$ six-subsets and each of the 5 colours, a width-15 clause "some edge inside this subset has this colour" ≈ 1.15M clauses. Large but plausible for a modern solver; expect thrash without symmetry breaking (full symmetry group is $S_{26}\times S_5$).
- Incremental-n strategy: solve K_n for n = 6, 7, … upward to locate the empirical SAT/UNSAT frontier before committing compute to n=26 directly. UNSAT at any n ≤ 26 settles the r=5 case (keep DRAT certificates).

## Attempts (append-only log: date, approach, outcome, why it failed or was abandoned)

- **2026-07-06..09 — UNSAT certification of the machine lemmas is out of reach on this hardware; measured, not guessed.** Data: (i) the r=4 *validation analogue* of MH″ (balanced K_16 + 3-set hitter; 480 variables) ran 68 CPU-hours on kissat with no verdict; (ii) the r=5 keystone (h4b, with a sound S_21 symmetry cut) ran 98 CPU-hours, no verdict; (iii) a 15-cube split thrashed in a swap storm (11.1/12 GB swap — 15 solvers × learned-clause growth exceed 64 GB); (iv) two CEGAR loops ran 8,366 and 2,339 rounds with violations-per-round oscillating 1–29, i.e. non-convergent lazy exclusion over a huge model space (their per-round caps totalled <5% of the 177k-set cap family); (v) two monolithic full-cap instances (80.8M and 105M clauses) were OOM-killed when run concurrently, and the solo restart also died silently. **Conclusion: deciding MH″/MM/L5′ by DRAT-certified UNSAT needs cluster-scale cube-and-conquer (march_cu-style splitting, thousands of cubes, ~10²–10³ CPU-days), or a fundamentally better encoding/WLOG. On the SAT side the story is closed empirically: witnesses exist through n=24 (verified) and five independent searches wall at exactly 24 residual violations at n=25 (RESULTS.md R6).** Local search demolishes CDCL on the SAT side of this family (n=23: locsearch minutes vs kissat >900s timeout; n=24: 80k steps warm-chained).

- **2026-07-05 — Lemma H (h_c ≥ 5 from graph constraints alone) is FALSE.** Conjectured: no graph on 21 vertices with α≤4 and 6-set cap ≤11 (would force every extension part |T_c| = 5). SAT found an 80-edge witness (tools/lemma_h.py, data/sat/lemma_h_21.log). Consequence: a colour class with ~80+ edges could in principle have hitting number 4, and 4-point T's evade the "must contain a c-edge" tightness argument. The chain survives only if h_c = 4 is impossible *inside an actual balanced colouring* — that is exactly probe_h4 (data/sat/r5_n25_h4.cnf): balanced K_25 + class 0 + fixed 4-set T = {0,1,2,3} with α(G_0 − T) ≤ 4. UNSAT restores the chain (Lemma H″); SAT yields exotic candidate colourings that evade the main obstruction (test extension immediately). Also computing μ(21) = min edges of such 21-vertex graphs (data/sat/mu21.log) for budget arguments.

- **2026-07-05 — CEGAR aux-variable collision produced a spurious UNSAT for the r=5 minority lemma (caught before recording).** First run of `tools/minority.py 26 5 65 --cegar` reported UNSAT after one round. Inspection showed the global ≤65 seqcounter's auxiliary variables were never reserved in the ID pool, so the lazily-added local totalizers aliased them — garbage constraints. Symptom that raised the alarm: adding caps on only 4 subsets flipped a large SAT space to UNSAT. Fixed (pool.top bump), rerun launched. **Lesson: for any SAT result that would settle the problem, demand (a) a second solver, (b) a second encoding, (c) a DRAT/LRAT certificate, and (d) plausibility checks on the witness/margin, before writing it anywhere.** True fact salvaged from the buggy run's round 1: (α≤5, K_6-free, ≤65 edges, n=26) alone is SAT — the pure Ramsey–Turán version of the minority lemma fails at r=5 just as it does at r=3; the 6-set density caps are load-bearing in the corrected lemma L5′.

- **2026-07-05 — Naive minority-class lemma (min edges of (K_6-free, α≤5) graphs on 26 vertices ≥ 66) — FALSE READING, caught by SAT.** I initially read ErGy's Lemmas 1–2 as pure Ramsey–Turán minimum-edge statements ("K_4-free + α≤3 on 10 vertices needs ≥16 edges"). SAT found a 15-edge witness, contradicting that reading. Rereading the paper: their proofs use K_4−e as well — the correct phenomenon is that an (r+1)-set with ≥ C(r+1,2)−(r−2) edges in ONE class sees ≤ r−1 colours. The corrected minority condition adds: every (r+1)-set spans ≤ C(r+1,2)−(r−1) edges of the class. With that cap the r=3 instance is UNSAT at 15 (= Lemma 1) and also at 16, 17 — ErGy's bound had ≥3 edges of slack at r=3. **Lesson: encode the exact statement, validate against known theorems before believing anything; the referee caught a subtle mis-formalization.** The corrected r=5 question (n=26, α≤5, 6-sets ≤ 11 edges, ≤ 65 edges total — UNSAT would settle r=5 via the minority argument) is running via CEGAR (tools/minority.py).

- **2026-07-05 — Extend the AG(2,5) merged colourings of K_25 by one vertex.** Outcome: impossible, all 15 merge choices (RESULTS.md R2, SAT-UNSAT via tools/extend.py). Why it fails (candidate proof, review_queue/ag-nonextension.md): monochromatic lines force each new-edge colour class to have exactly 5 points and, for unmerged colours, to *be* a line of the exempt direction; four such lines are pairwise non-parallel hence pairwise intersecting — but colour classes are disjoint. The obstruction generalizes to every prime power r. **Consequence: disproving the conjecture at r=5 requires either a non-AG balanced colouring of K_25 (existence unknown) or a K_26 colouring found directly; the textbook construction is a dead end for W1.**
